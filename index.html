For the Love of Data: A Story

A couple owns a small, modest store that they've run for decades. They've worked tirelessly, day in and day out, to make ends meet. Over the years, they managed to save enough money to send their only son, David, to college. Proud of his parents' sacrifice, David studied hard and graduated with a degree in accounting.

Excited to put his new knowledge to use, David offers to help his parents with the store's finances. David spends hours poring over the the old, worn ledger, calculating, analyzing, and double-checking everything. Finally, he comes to his parents with a concerned expression.

"Mom, Dad," David begins hesitantly, "The store is losing money! You need to make some serious changes, or you might have to close the store."

We're not about to change things just because some accountant says so.

His parents look at each other and then at David, a mixture of amusement and disbelief on their faces.

His father chuckles, "David, we've been running this store for 30 years. It put you through college, didn't it? Now you come back with your fancy college degree and tell us we're losing money?"

"But Dad," David protests, "I'm telling you, these numbers don't lie. The expenses are higher than the income!"

His father replies, "This store's been good to us for 30 years. We're not about to change things just because some accountant says so. After all, it's this 'losing money' store that got you through college, isn't it?"
